About Me

Thank you for visiting my website. My name is Charlie Sanchez, and I am a junior at the Wake Early College of Information and Biotechnologies. My path of study is computer programming/IT in general, which I enjoy but do not plan to pursue in the future. I have learned about networking, Python, and web design alongside other skills. I used to have interest in computer forensics, but have shifted interest from there. This school provides an incredible community, and I enjoy working with my peers. It has allowed me to explore the IT field, something I wouldn't have delved into if not for this school. There is also a strong emphasis on college and career readiness, my classes aiding me in developing useful soft skills.

Shifting away from school, I have a lot of interests and passions I pursue in my free time. I am extremely passionate about the arts - I love acting, dancing, singing, writing, music, and visual art. Many of these interests bleed into my persona at school - I am the president of the drama department and I am a strong competitor on our debate team. I am also a member of the game development club, fusing my technical skills with my love for art and design. My creativity and desire to innovate motivates me to become an engineer - notably, a nuclear engineer. Nuclear energy is a topic I just recently discovered, but have fallen in love with. Developing a completely clean energy source like nuclear fusion is one of my main goals in life. Powering an entire world and reducing the fear around nuclear is something I would love to achieve.

I also want to keep pursuing my artistic interests in the future. I want to become a somewhat recognized actress, a musician (I'm currently learning the guitar, but I would love to learn the harpsichord and accordion!), and an artist. I also really want to live in Germany, so I've been learning German these past few months. It is such a beautiful language... I am planning to go to a four-year college after high school, and I may even chase a master's degree afterwards. I will probably stay local until I secure an overseas job, which will hopefully be soon after I graduate college!
